HISTORY OF THE INTERNET The Internet has its origins in the Cold War. In 1957, the Soviet Union launched the first satellite, Sputnik. In response, the United States created the Advanced Research Projects Agency, or ARPA. The plan emerged to create a communication network that would not be vulnerable to a nuclear attack In 1969, ARPA awarded contracts for the development of the arpanet project, which adopted packet switching technology. The network of Interface Message Processors was built, and the host-to-host protocol was specified by a group of graduate students at UCLA. Arpanet adopted packet switching technology, connecting computers across the United States and eventually reaching the United Kingdom and Norway In the 1970s, other packet-switched networks emerged where there was a simplified end-to-end approach to internetworking, where it was put into practice. The development behind Ethernet and PARC Universal Packet was developed by BOB later on .
